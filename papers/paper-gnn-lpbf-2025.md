# Graph neural networks enable design of high-performance aluminum alloys for laser-based additive manufacturing

**Venue:** Virtual and Physical Prototyping
**Year:** 2025
**DOI:** [10.1080/17452759.2025.2549366](https://doi.org/10.1080/17452759.2025.2549366)
**Cited by:** 1
**Affiliation:** Monash University (per Kimi report attribution)

## One-line takeaway
Graph Attention Network (GAT) combined with a physical-metallurgy knowledge
graph + genetic algorithm discovered KG-AMAA, a new Al alloy for LPBF with
superior strength-ductility synergy vs existing AM aluminum alloys.

## Method
- **Framework:** GAT + physical-metallurgy knowledge graph + GA
- **Output:** New alloy "KG-AMAA" tailored for laser powder bed fusion
- **Microstructure:** Al3Sc + AlSc2Si2 + Si eutectic + Al2Cu strengthening phases
- **Result:** Superior strength-ductility synergy vs existing AM Al alloys

## Why it matters
- First GNN + knowledge-graph approach for Al alloy design
- Knowledge graph encodes physical-metallurgy rules (which phases form, how they
  strengthen) as constraints
- Demonstrates GAT works for alloy design (not just property prediction)

## Open opportunity
- Generalize to other alloy families (high-entropy, Mg, Ti, Cu)
- Couple GAT with industrial trial loop
- Open the knowledge graph as a community resource
