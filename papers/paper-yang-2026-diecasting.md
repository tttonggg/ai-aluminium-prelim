# Ultra-fast design and application of non-heat-treatable integrated die casting aluminum alloys

**Authors:** Dong Yang, Junying Min, Wang Yi, Jianbao Gao, Tianchuang Gao
**Venue:** npj Computational Materials
**Year:** 2026
**DOI:** [10.1038/s41524-026-02010-3](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41524-026-02010-3)

**Open Access:** Yes (npj is OA by default)

## One-line takeaway
ICME-driven (Materials Genome Initiative) design of a new non-heat-treatable Al
alloy for large integrated die-cast structural parts — compresses the
traditional 5-10 year alloy-development cycle.

## Method
- Computation-driven design (CALPHAD + ICME)
- Targets large complex integrated die-cast structural parts
- Non-heat-treatable = no T6 / T7 step → lower cost, faster production

## Why it matters
- Directly addresses the *die casting* process (one of the user's two focus areas)
- Non-heat-treatable alloys are critical for large integrated parts (EV battery
  housings, structural components)
- ICME + MGI methodology shows the speed-up: years → months
