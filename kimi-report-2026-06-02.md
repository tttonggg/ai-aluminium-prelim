7.1 PIML FOR SDAS IN RECYCLED AL-SI (MDPI Machines, 2026)
----------------------------------------------------------
PROBLEM: Standard FVM simulations ignore batch-to-batch chemical variations in 
recycled aluminum, leading to SDAS misprediction.

FRAMEWORK:
• SDAS_corrected = SDAS_FVM × (1 + δ_solute)
• δ_ML learned by Gradient Boosting Regressor
• Physics: SDAS = K·Ṫ^(-n), n ≈ 0.33–0.5; Q = Σ m_i(k_i-1)C_0,i

INPUTS: t_sol, Q_total, (Fe, Si, Mg, Ti) wt%
TARGET: δ = P_physics(Q, Ṫ) − M_FVM(Ṫ)

RESULTS:
• R² = 0.95 (test), MAE = 0.24 μm with synthetic noise (σ ≈ 0.25 μm)
• Feature importance: Solidification time 51%, Q factor 46%
• Key finding: Ti variations drive 25% non-linear SDAS reduction
• Ti threshold effect: Refinement up to ~0.1 wt%, reversal above due to 
  (Al,Si)3Ti phase formation

SIGNIFICANCE: First hard-constraint PIML that "injects" solute physics into 
commercial FVM solvers via learned residual correction.

9. CRITICAL KNOWLEDGE GAPS
================================================================================

1. HOT TEARING PREDICTION
   • Limited ML studies specifically targeting hot tearing susceptibility
   • Current approach: FE simulation + empirical criteria (RDG, Clyne-Kurz)
   • Opportunity: ML models predicting HTI from composition + process parameters

2. MACROSEGREGATION MODELING
   • Few ML models predict macrosegregation patterns
   • Current: Traditional numerical simulation (FVM, meshless methods)
   • Gap: Real-time prediction of composition variation across ingot cross-section

3. DIE WEAR PREDICTION
   • Limited research on ML-based die life prediction
   • Economic impact: Die replacement costs 15–30% of extrusion operating costs
   • Opportunity: Image-based + sensor fusion models for die condition monitoring

4. CLOSED-LOOP AUTONOMOUS CONTROL
   • Gap between predictive models and autonomous process control
   • Current: Human-in-the-loop with ML recommendations
   • Future: Reinforcement learning agents directly controlling press parameters

5. STANDARDIZED BENCHMARKS
   • No standardized datasets or evaluation protocols
   • Prevents fair comparison across studies
   • Need: Open datasets for X-ray defects, die designs, alloy properties

6. UNCERTAINTY QUANTIFICATION
   • Most studies report point estimates only
   • Need: Calibrated prediction intervals, epistemic + aleatoric uncertainty
   • Methods: Bayesian neural networks, deep ensembles, conformal prediction

7. TRANSFER LEARNING ACROSS ALLOY SYSTEMS
   • Limited exploration of pre-trained models
   • Opportunity: Foundation models for aluminum alloys (like GPT for language)
   • Challenge: Domain shift between alloy families (2xxx vs 7xxx vs Al-Si)

8. REAL-TIME MICROSTRUCTURE PREDICTION
   • Current: Post-process microscopy or simulation-based prediction
   • Gap: In-situ prediction during casting/extrusion for immediate adjustment
   • Opportunity: Sensor fusion + ML for online microstructure estimation
