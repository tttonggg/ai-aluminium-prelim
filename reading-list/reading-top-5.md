# Top 5 papers (highest leverage)

1. **Karniadakis et al. (2021), *Physics-informed machine learning*,
   Nature Reviews Physics** — DOI: `10.1038/s42254-021-00314-5`
   - The single best framing paper for ML + physics work
   - 6,362 citations; foundational reading for any PINN-adjacent work

2. **Choudhary et al. (2022), *Recent advances and applications of deep
   learning methods in materials science*, npj Computational Materials**
   — DOI: `10.1038/s41524-022-00734-6`
   - Comprehensive methods review, organized by data type
   - 1,036 citations

3. **Schmidt et al. (2019), *Recent advances and applications of machine
   learning in solid-state materials science*, npj Computational Materials**
   — DOI: `10.1038/s41524-019-0221-0`
   - The earlier landmark methods review; complements Choudhary
   - 2,366 citations

4. **Kotadia et al. (2021), *A review of Laser Powder Bed Fusion AM of
   aluminium alloys: Microstructure, defects, post-processing, mechanical
   properties*, Additive Manufacturing** — DOI: `10.1016/j.addma.2021.102155`
   - The clearest Al-AM landscape, sets context for any Al work
   - 469 citations

5. **Guo et al. (2024), *Numerical Simulation and Machine Learning Prediction
   of the Direct Chill Casting Process*, Materials**
   — DOI: `10.3390/ma17061409`
   - The most directly on-topic for DC casting + ML

# If you have a day, also read

- **Raissi et al. (2019), *Physics-informed neural networks*, J. Comp. Physics**
  — DOI: `10.1016/j.jcp.2018.10.045` (16,251 cit) — the PINN paper
- **Cuomo et al. (2022), *Scientific Machine Learning through PINNs***
  — DOI: `10.1007/s10915-022-01939-z` (2,225 cit) — modern PINN review
- **Tabor et al. (2018), *Accelerating the discovery of materials for clean
  energy in the era of smart automation*, Nature Reviews Materials**
  — DOI: `10.1038/s41578-018-0005-z` (775 cit)
- **Pak & Barati Farimani (2025), AdditiveLLM** — arXiv `2501.17784`
- **Dagdelen et al. (2024), *Structured information extraction from scientific
  text with large language models***, Nature Communications
  — DOI: `10.1038/s41467-024-45563-x` (549 cit)
